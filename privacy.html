Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026 • version 2.1

1. Introduction & Scope

Avera ("Avera", "we", "us", "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website (the "Site"), make a purchase, or interact with our services. Please read this policy carefully. By using Avera, you consent to the data practices described herein.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect both personally identifiable information (PII) and non-personal data about you in various ways, including:

  • Account & Order Information: Name, email address, shipping/billing address, phone number, payment details (processed securely via third-party gateways).
  • Browsing Data: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages visited, time spent, and referring URLs via cookies and analytics tools.
  • Communications: Any correspondence you send to us via email, chat, or contact forms.
  • Marketing Preferences: Your subscription status, preferences for newsletters, and responses to surveys.
Sensitive data: We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (e.g., health or biometric information). Payment card information is never stored on our servers — it is tokenized by our PCI-DSS compliant payment processor.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected data for legitimate business purposes, including:

  • Processing and fulfilling your orders, including shipping, returns, and customer support.
  • Improving our Site, products, and user experience through analytics and personalization.
  • Sending transactional emails (order confirmations, shipping updates, password resets).
  • Marketing communications where you have provided consent (e.g., newsletters, early access to collections).
  • Detecting fraud, security incidents, and legal compliance.

4. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Avera uses cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for site functionality (cart, checkout).
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our Site (via services like Google Analytics).
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your language and region settings.
  • Marketing Cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements and measure campaign performance.

You may adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies, but some features of the Site may not work properly. By continuing to use Avera, you consent to our use of cookies as described.

5. Sharing Your Information

We never sell or rent your personal data to third parties. However, we may share information with trusted service providers who assist us in operating our business, subject to confidentiality agreements:

  • Shipping carriers (e.g., USPS, DHL, FedEx) to deliver your order.
  • Payment processors (Stripe, PayPal) to securely handle transactions.
  • Email & marketing platforms (Klaviyo, Mailchimp) to manage newsletters.
  • Analytics providers (Google Analytics, Triple Whale) to analyze site traffic.
  • Legal & regulatory authorities if required by law or to protect our rights.

6. Data Retention & Security

We retain your personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, including legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. For example, order records are kept for 7 years to comply with tax laws. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures — including encryption, firewalls, and access controls — to protect your data from unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your responsibility: You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account password and for any activity under your account.

7. Your Privacy Rights (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data. These may include:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your data, subject to legal obligations.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data.
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: We do not sell your data, but you may opt out of targeted advertising via cookie settings.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@Avera-editions.com. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

8. Third-Party Links

Our Site may contain links to external websites (e.g., maker profiles, social media). This Privacy Policy does not apply to those third-party sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

9. Children's Privacy

Avera does not knowingly collect or solicit personal information from anyone under the age of 16. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately. We will take steps to delete such information.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. The revised version will be indicated by an updated "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Your continued use of Avera after any changes constitutes acceptance of the new Privacy Policy.

11. Contact Us

If you have questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data handling practices, please reach out to our Data Protection team:

Email: privacy@Avera-editions.com
Postal Address: Avera Privacy Office, 47 Bridge Street, Brooklyn, NY 11201, USA

Your trust is our highest priority. We are committed to transparency and responsible data stewardship.
